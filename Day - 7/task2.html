Ingredients

  1. For rice
    1. 2 cups raw rice
    2. 4 cups water
    3. ½ cup toor dal
    4. 1½–2 cups water
    5. Pinch of salt
    6. 1 lemon-sized ball tamarind
    7. 2 medium ripe tomatoes, chopped
    8. 1½–2 tbsp rasam powder
    9. 1 tsp jaggery or sugar
    10. 2–3 cups water
    11. 1 tbsp ghee or oil
    12. 1 tsp mustard seeds
    13. ½ tsp cumin seeds
    14. A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
    15. 8–10 curry leaves
    16. 2–3 garlic cloves crushed
    17. 1–2 dried red chilies broken
    18. 1Fresh coriander leaves, chopped
    19. 2 tbsp coriander seeds
    20. 1 tbsp chana dal
    21. 1 tsp cumin seeds
    22. 1 tsp black peppercorns
    23. 2–3 dry red chilies

Equipment

  1. Equipment
    1. Pressure cooker or pot for dal
    2. Medium saucepan for rasam
    3. Heavy pot or rice cooker for rice

Step-by-Step Procedure

  1. Cook rice
    1. Rinse 2 cups rice until water runs fairly clear. (Optional: soak 15–20 minutes.)
    2. Cook with 4 cups water on stove or rice cooker until fluffy. Keep covered and warm
  2. Cook the toor dal
    1. Rinse ½ cup toor dal. Pressure cook with 1½–2 cups water and a pinch of turmeric for 3–4 whistles (or simmer 20–30 min until soft).
    2. Mash lightly with a spoon so it’s semi-smooth. Reserve ~1 cup of the dal water (strainer if needed) — you’ll use this to adjust consistency.
  3. Extract tamarind
    1. Soak a lemon-sized ball of tamarind in 1 cup warm water for 10 minutes; squeeze and strain to get about ¾ cup tamarind extract. (Or dissolve 2 tbsp tamarind paste in ¾ cup warm water.)
    2. Strain to remove fibers and seeds; set the tamarind extract aside.
  4. Prepare rasam base
    1. Heat 1 tbsp ghee or oil in a medium saucepan. Add 1 tsp mustard seeds; when they pop add ½ tsp cumin seeds (optional), a pinch of asafoetida (hing), 8–10 curry leaves, crushed garlic (if using), and 1–2 broken dried red chilies. Sauté 20–30 seconds.
    2. Add 2 medium chopped tomatoes and ¼ tsp turmeric. Cook until tomatoes soften and start to break down (about 3–5 minutes).
    3. Pour in the reserved tamarind extract plus 1–1½ cups water. Stir in 1½–2 tbsp rasam powder and salt to taste. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 5–7 minutes to meld flavors.
  5. Add dal and finish
    1. Add the lightly mashed toor dal and about ½–1 cup of the reserved dal water (adjust to desired consistency). Mix well and simmer 2–3 minutes.
    2. Taste and adjust seasoning: add more salt, a pinch of jaggery if too sour, or a squeeze of lemon. If too thick, add hot water to achieve a thin, soup-like rasam.
  6. Final tempering (tadka)
    1. Heat 1 tsp ghee in a small pan. Add ½ tsp mustard seeds and a few curry leaves; when they pop, pour this hot tempering over the rasam.
    2. Garnish with chopped fresh coriander leaves.
  7. Assemble rasam rice
    1. Place 1–1½ cups cooked rice in a bowl or plate.
    2. Pour ¾–1 cup hot rasam over the rice (adjust to taste). Add a small knob of ghee or a drizzle of sesame oil if desired.
    3. Mix well and serve hot with papad, pickle, or a vegetable side.
  8. Tips & Variations
    1. Pepper rasam: increase crushed black pepper and reduce rasam powder for a spicier, medicinal version.
    2. Garlic rasam: add extra crushed garlic in the tempering or rasam base.
    3. Quick version: use tamarind paste and store-bought rasam powder to save time.
  9. Storage & Reheat
    1. Store leftover rasam (without rice) in the refrigerator for 2–3 days.
    2. Reheat gently on the stove and add water if it thickens. Avoid prolonged boiling after adding tamarind or lemon to preserve flavor.
